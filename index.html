Ytre Eikjo

Introduction, the farm.

Ytre Eikjo is an idyllic roadless farm in a fjord in Norway. That belonged to my great-great-grandfather Mons Eikum, and he was the last farmer on Ytre Eikjo before he started to split it up into smaller farms. Two of those are still in the family today, and the family cabin that I have with my brother and my sister is on one of them. It is a special place for me, and perhaps it will be a place that arouses your curiosity. I will at least try to gather here some of the historic pieces of this place.

The Farm is one of a total of four in Eikjastrondi ¹, in Luster municipality. The source says that Eikjastrondi is a grend, the closest denotation for this in English would be a hamlet. I feel that the farms are too scattered for being called that. But it is at least an area that extends from Notedalen (Notedal) in the northeast to Ytre Eikjo in the South-west. With a road that goes to Vedviki (Vedvik) via Notedalen. Then it is a little over a kilometer of rugged terrain to Ytre Eikjo, where all traffic has to go by boats. In the other direction, there are about five kilometers to the nearest road in the village of Solvorn.
